Chicken Mole

Home

Description

Mole is a complex and spicy Mexican sauce made with a number of ingredients that vary from region to region. It can be used for enchiladas, tacos, or to top vegetables or meat. This version is fairly spicy and nut forward, but the ratio of nuts to other ingredient can be reduced per personal preference.



Chile Paste

  • 75g ancho chiles
  • 45g pasilla chiles
  • 30g chipotle chiles
  • warm water

Chicken

  • 1500g boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 2 bay leaves

Mole Sauce

  • 170g tomato paste
  • 80g sesame seeds
  • 100g peanuts
  • 30g pecans
  • 105g raisins
  • 20g cocoa powder
  • 10g granulated sugar
  • 1g cinnamon
  • 1 medium onion, cut into quarters
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • salt to taste


Instructions

Chile Paste

  1. Soak dried ancho, pasilla, and chipotle peppers in hot water for 15 minutes until soft.
  2. Drain water from chiles, reserving a small amount for blending.
  3. Blend until smooth.

Chicken Precooking

  1. Add chicken thighs and bay leaves to Instant Pot and pressure cook on high pressure for 10 minutes, allowing natural pressure release.
  2. Remove chicken, reserving 1.5L of cooking liquid, and shred or cut into pieces.

Mole Sauce

  1. Cover raisins with hot water.
  2. Toast peanuts, pecans, and sesame seeds in a dry pan. Reserve.
  3. Lightly oil pan and heat on medium-high heat. Place onion pieces cut side down and cook undisturbed until dark brown.
  4. Add crushed garlic and cook for 1-2 more minutes until fragrant.
  5. Add tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes stirring constantly to avoid burning.
  6. Add cinnamon, peanuts, pecans, sesame seeds, raisins, and sugar.
  7. Add chicken cooking water and cook, stirring until boiling.
  8. Blend with immersion blender until it reaches desired consistency, adding more chicken cooking water if necessary.
  9. Add sauce to container with shredded chicken and stir until combined.
  10. Garnish with additional sesame seeds and serve with tortillas or rice!
